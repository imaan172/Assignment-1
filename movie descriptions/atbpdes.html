All the Bright Places

Directed by Brett Haley

Released on 28 February 2020
Starring: Elle Fanning and Justice Smith

Movie Description

The film follows the lives of two high school students, Violet Markey and Theodore Finch, both struggling with emotional and psychological issues. Violet is coping with the loss of her sister in a car accident, which has left her feeling disconnected and unable to enjoy life. Theodore, on the other hand, is known as a "freak" at school due to his unpredictable behavior and his own battles with depression.

Violet and Theodore's paths cross when they meet on the ledge of a bell tower, contemplating suicide. They talk each other down from the edge and form an unexpected bond. Together, they embark on a journey to explore the beauty and wonder of their home state of Indiana by visiting unique places and leaving behind "bright places."

As their relationship deepens, Violet and Theodore fall in love and provide support and understanding for each other's struggles. However, their journey is marked by the challenges of mental health, and they must confront their own demons.

The film sensitively portrays the complexities of love, friendship, and mental health issues, highlighting the importance of reaching out for help and finding bright spots even in the darkest of times. It's a story of two young souls who help each other heal and find hope amidst their personal struggles.
